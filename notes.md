# My notes
{: .no_toc }

Everything you have highlighted, noted, or flagged while reading, plus how far through the
course you are.

> **This lives in your browser, not on a server.** There is no account and nothing is uploaded.
> Your highlights are stored in this browser's `localStorage`, which means they are private to
> you, and also that they will not follow you to another machine and will be lost if you clear
> site data. Use **Export JSON** below to keep a copy, and **Import JSON** to restore it or move
> it elsewhere.

---

## How it works
{: .no_toc }

**Highlighting.** Select any text in a unit. A small toolbar appears with three choices:

| Choice | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Highlight** | Marks the passage in yellow. No comment. |
| **Note…** | Marks it and attaches a comment. |
| **Flag error…** | Marks it in red as something you believe is *wrong* — a sign error, a bad citation, a derivation that does not follow. |

Click any existing highlight to edit its note, switch it between note and error, report it, or
delete it.

**Errors become GitHub issues.** Anything flagged as an error gets a **Report on GitHub** button,
here and on the page itself. It opens a prefilled issue against
[the repository](https://github.com/stootoon/circuits-to-algorithms/issues) with the page link,
the quoted passage, and your comment, labelled `erratum`. You review it before submitting —
nothing is filed automatically. Given that a good deal of this course consists of derivations
that have not been checked by anyone but me, this is the single most useful thing you can do
with it.

**Progress.** Each unit has a *Mark complete* button under its title. Completed units get a ✓ in
the sidebar and count toward the meter at the bottom of the navigation panel.

**Panels.** The **◀ Hide** button top-left collapses the whole navigation panel for wide-format
reading; section groups in the sidebar collapse individually, and both remember their state
between pages.

A caveat worth knowing: highlights are anchored to the *text* you selected, not to a position in
the file. If I later edit a sentence you highlighted, that highlight will no longer find its
home and will quietly disappear from the page — though it stays in this list, so nothing is
lost. Passages with equations in them anchor on the surrounding prose, since the rendered
mathematics is not plain text.
