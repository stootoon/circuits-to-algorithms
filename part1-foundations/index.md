# Phase 1 — Foundations

Six methodological modules. These are the tools; Phase 2 is where you watch them being used.

The modules split into the two master strategies of
[Orientation §2.4](../00-orientation/README.md): Units 01–02 are **bottom-up** — reduce the
dynamics until a recognizable primitive falls out. Units 03–04 and 06 are **top-down** — posit
an objective, derive the optimal solution, check it against the circuit. Unit 05 is the hinge:
a learning rule is a mechanism, but identifying the objective it implicitly descends is a
normative act.

Run both strategies on every circuit you care about. The conversions that have lasted are the
ones where they met in the middle.

| Unit | Title | What it gives you |
|---|---|---|
| [01](01-dynamical-systems.md) | Dynamical systems as the lingua franca | Phase portraits as the algorithm; fixed-point reverse-engineering |
| [02](02-population-geometry.md) | The linear algebra and geometry of populations | Latent manifolds; low-rank connectivity; shattering dimensionality |
| [03](03-efficient-coding.md) | Efficient coding: deriving circuits from principles | Running the normative derivation *forward* |
| [04](04-probabilistic-computation.md) | Circuits that represent posteriors | Parametric vs. sampling codes, and how to tell them apart |
| [05](05-learning-rules.md) | Learning rules as the extracted algorithm | Rule → implied objective |
| [06](06-control-and-filtering.md) | Control, filtering, and internal models | "The circuit subtracts a prediction" |

**If you are short on time:** 01 and 02 are non-negotiable — nearly every case study uses them.
03 is the one that teaches you to *derive* rather than *describe*. 04–06 can be read on demand
when a case study calls for them.

---

These are the longest units in the course, by design: they carry the derivations that the case
studies then use without re-deriving. Each ends with a **Mathematical structure spotlight**
pointing into the [structures thread](../structures/README.md), an **Exercises** section of nine
to ten problems tagged ★/★★/★★★, and a **Solutions** section below it with every problem worked
in full. The exercises are deliberately not interleaved with their solutions here — work a set
before you check it.
